# MONEY MACHINE BLUEPRINT

## The uncomfortable truth
A repo does not make money. A narrow offer with a painful problem does. TITANIUS_CERBERRUS should therefore be positioned as an orchestration core that ships sellable workflow products.

## Positioning
**TITANIUS_CERBERRUS** = a modular revenue operations engine for solo founders, creators, and small businesses that lose money because they do not follow up, do not validate offers, and do not turn scattered signals into action.

## Initial buyer segments

### 1. Solo founders
Pain:
- too many ideas, no scoring
- inconsistent follow-up
- weak offer validation

What to sell:
- Offer Validation Engine
- Lead Recovery Engine
- Weekly Opportunity Brief

### 2. Creators with small audiences
Pain:
- audience exists but does not convert
- no product ladder
- no signal on what to launch next

What to sell:
- Creator Revenue OS
- Niche analysis and offer scoring
- launch decision reports

### 3. Local service businesses
Pain:
- missed leads
- late replies
- quote follow-up chaos
- poor reactivation of old prospects

What to sell:
- Follow-up Recovery Workflow
- Lost Lead Reactivation Workflow
- Weekly pipeline health report

## Product ladder

### Tier 1: One-off audit
**Name:** Revenue Leak Audit
**Price:** 99 to 249 EUR
**Deliverable:** PDF or markdown report with prioritized fixes
**Goal:** fast cash and customer discovery

### Tier 2: Setup service
**Name:** Lead Recovery Setup
**Price:** 300 to 900 EUR
**Deliverable:** configured workflow, templates, scoring rules, weekly report
**Goal:** first serious revenue and proof of utility

### Tier 3: Subscription
**Name:** TITANIUS Revenue Ops
**Price:** 79 to 299 EUR per month
**Deliverable:** recurring workflow execution, reports, alerts, recommendation engine
**Goal:** compounding monthly revenue

### Tier 4: Premium custom build
**Name:** Custom Agentic Workflow Stack
**Price:** 1500+ EUR
**Deliverable:** tailored workflow system for a niche business
**Goal:** cash injections and case studies

## What the MVP must do
The first version should not try to do everything. It should do one painful thing very well.

### Recommended MVP
**Lead Recovery Engine**

Input:
- CSV or JSON lead list
- status fields
- last contact date
- source
- estimated deal value

Logic:
- score stale leads
- rank best recovery opportunities
- recommend follow-up priority
- output message templates or next actions

Output:
- JSON report
- markdown summary
- recovery queue sorted by score

Why this first:
- easy to explain
- easy to demo
- easy to sell
- directly tied to money

## Core metrics
Track only metrics that relate to revenue:
- leads recovered
- response rate after reactivation
- meetings booked
- estimated pipeline value recovered
- monthly recurring revenue
- time to first value

## Distribution strategy

### Fastest path
1. create one clean offer page
2. produce 3 demo reports from sample data
3. contact 20 prospects in one niche
4. sell manual plus automated setup before building anything fancy
5. use customer feedback to decide next workflow

### Best early niches
- coaches and consultants
- local service operators
- small agencies
- creators selling digital products
- solo SaaS founders

## Message that sells
Do not sell “AI agents.” Sell the result.

Bad:
- autonomous orchestration platform
- modular agentic architecture

Good:
- recover lost leads automatically
- rank the opportunities most likely to close
- turn messy prospect lists into a weekly money queue

## Build order
1. CLI and workflow registry
2. lead recovery workflow
3. scoring config
4. JSON and markdown reporting
5. sample datasets
6. smoke tests
7. simple landing page
8. Stripe checkout or payment link
9. case studies
10. second workflow only after first revenue

## Kill criteria
If the first 10 outreach conversations do not produce strong interest in lead recovery, pivot the workflow, not the whole repository.

## Success definition
Success is not stars on GitHub. Success is:
- first paying customer
- repeatable problem-solution fit
- reusable workflow core
- recurring revenue from a narrow use case
